# .impeccable.md

Design context for Ju Chen's personal portfolio (1021ju.github.io).

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, founders, and collaborators evaluating Ju for AI Marketing / Content Strategy / Growth roles (or freelance engagements). Most will spend 20–60 seconds on first pass. A smaller second group — people who linked in from her viral demos — will read longer.

### Brand Personality
Bookish, shipping, opinionated. She writes like someone who reads philosophy in the morning and pushes weird JS demos at 2am. The portfolio should feel *made by a person with taste*, not *generated*.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial, artifact-first, light/warm. Cream paper background (not white), warm ink text (not pure black), one serious accent (fountain-pen indigo). Typography carries the weight — distinctive display serif paired with a journalistic grotesk. Numbered sections like a zine. Zero cards nested in cards. Work entries are short essays with live links, not metric tiles.

**References in spirit:** NYT Sunday Magazine page design, small-press philosophy zines, a curious-mind's field notebook.

**Anti-references:** the default AI-marketer portfolio — dark mode + Inter + AI purple + nested cards + "Hi, I'm [name]" + metric tiles.

### Design Principles
1. **The artifact is the pitch.** A marketer with a template portfolio loses instantly. The site itself must demonstrate taste.
2. **Work is essays with links, not cards with metrics.** Every numbered entry links to something live or shipped.
3. **Typography over ornament.** No decorative stripes, gradients, glassmorphism. Hierarchy comes from type scale and whitespace.
4. **Numbers as prose, not tiles.** A metric earned inline in a sentence hits harder than a metric boxed in a grid cell.
5. **Warm, not cool.** Cream + ink + one indigo accent. Never dark-mode-as-tech-performance.
