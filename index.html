Chapter I  ·  The Brief

I was hired to build an AI-for-Science brand from nearly zero.

September 2025, Bohrium (DP Technology). What existed: one X account, one LinkedIn, no SEO, no Blog, no English content system — and 170 million academic papers waiting to be translated into attention. What follows is eight months of what that looked like in numbers.

The shape above was produced by a single person running LinkedIn, X (four accounts), Blog, Medium, and Reddit in parallel, with a full-stack content engine of my own building — a prompt system for SEO, a Conference-Hub pipeline, a rotation of interactive demos, a 29-skill Claude-Code agent stack. The rest of this page is how.

Chapter II  ·  The Playbook

Seven things I built, and why each one mattered.

I treat a growth engine as seven parts that have to work together: a hook that makes people feel something, a gauntlet that makes them stay, a data story that earns trust, an automation that removes single-point-of-failure, an SEO spine that compounds, a narrative channel that scales, and an agent stack so one person can keep it all running. Here they are in that order.

I
The hook that makes people laugh

PhD Graveyard — Qingming Edition

~70% of blog traffic 1,315 users in 3 days Shared flowers via Cloudflare KV

The one you just played. 95 English gravestones; 123 Chinese ones for Qingming with burning paper, golden ingots, and a boy-on-an-ox meandering a W shape across the hills. Shipped into the Tomb-Sweeping window when everyone was thinking about death anyway.

Three days, ~70% of Blog traffic; peak ~1,200 views / 1,315 unique users. It worked because people got the joke immediately and still had a reason to come back.

II
The game that makes them stay

Paper or Bullshit?

203 questions live 15-second round timer Soft login after round two

Can you tell a real arXiv paper from one an LLM made up? 153 real titles against 50 generated ones, 15-second timer per round, soft login on round two, every real paper links straight into Bohrium. 203 questions in production; the tricky ones sit near 50% accuracy — the uncomfortable number we wanted.

The dataviz that earns trust

Research Stock Market

Bloomberg-terminal framing Real Bohrium paper data 50 SEO pages queued

A Bloomberg-terminal-styled visualization of academic disciplines as tradeable stocks — OHLC candlesticks, analyst ratings, a real-time ticker tape. The aggregate Hub page is live; fifty programmatic SEO pages (one per discipline, targeting existential searches like “Is X dying?”) are next in the build, ahead of the public launch on X.

IV
The automation that scales

Conference Hub pipeline

5 custom skills 5,356 papers triaged 2,300+ impressions in 48h

I judged single-paper write-ups would always be long-tail low-traffic, so I designed a conference Hub aggregation page as the actual traffic driver and built the five-Claude-Code-Skill pipeline behind it: OpenReview scraper → PDF fetcher → figure extractor → Bohrium lookup → article writer. ICLR 2026 processed 5,356 papers down to 53 selected; its Hub cleared 2,300+ impressions in 48 hours. AAAI's Hub has since reached 6,371.

Read the AAAI hub
V
The SEO spine that compounds

Blog engine + i18n architecture

23K search impressions Avg. Google position 6 English blog + Chinese playground split

The stack behind bohrium.com/blog, built from zero: a meta-title/description prompt system modelled on emergentmind, an eight-module article template, a SciencePedia keyword-injection layer, batch refresh scripts. When the engineering pipeline proved unstable I took over the visual redesign (Hugo templates + SCSS), ran the Technical SEO audit (JSON-LD, OG tags, canonicals), and eventually led the i18n architecture migration — splitting one Hugo site into an English research Blog (/en/blog/) and a Chinese interactive Playground (/playground/), with the multi-site login scheme and 301 redirect strategy.

Two months after launch: average Google position 6, 23K impressions.

VI
The narrative channel, revived

Medium, from 20 to 812 / month

40.6x overall multiplier CTO-approved editorial reset Audience-segmented templates

Took over Medium when it was running on AI-generated drafts pushing ~20 presentations/month. I rebalanced the technical-vs-popular line, got CTO sign-off on the new voice, then built reusable article structures and a rotation of style templates keyed to Medium's audience segments.

VII
The agent stack behind all of it

Three agents, twenty-nine skills

3 agent systems 29 self-built skills One-person content operating system

Three independent Claude Code agents — visual-brand, write-blog, conference-roundup — each with its own persistent knowledge base, collectively orchestrating 29 custom skills across 6 categories (paper selection, social, blog, SEO, conference pipeline, brand visuals).
